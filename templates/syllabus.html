Course Syllabus

General information from the syllabus is provided below. The Full PDF Version is also available to download.


Grading Scheme and Policies

Assignments: 22% — There are a total of 3 assignments. More information is available on the assignments page.

Tutorials/Labs: 4% — Marks will be given at the end of the semester.

Midterm Exam: 20% — Midterm information is available on the tests page.

Quizzes: 8% — There are a total of 4 quizzes. More information is available on the quizzes page.

Final Exam: 46% — Final exam information is available on the tests page.


Assignments

  • You may be asked to work in pairs or groups for any of these three assignments.
  • Please DO NOT email your assignments to us. We will be using MarkUs for the submission of all assignments.
  • All assignments will be marked on the UTSC Lab machines. Before submitting your assignments, make sure that they compile and execute on the UTSC Lab machines.
  • A strict silent policy takes effect 12 hours before all exams and assignment due dates. That means no question about the assignments will be answered by the instructor or TAs whether it is in person, via email, on the discussion board, or any other platform.

Quizzes

Once released, each quiz will be available to work on for about 24 hours or less. Depending on the quiz, you will have about 45 - 60 minutes to complete the quiz. Quizzes will not happen in lecture; you will be able to work on these quizzes outside the classroom. Explicit instructions will be made available on how these quizzes will be submitted.


Tutorials/Labs

Tutorials are a great way to get hands-on experience on many topics covered in the course.

Attendance is mandatory for tutorials. On some occasions, the TA will take attendance and assign small exercises for you to complete. All tutorials will begin from the 2nd week of the semester.


Final Exam

The final exam will have a length of 2 (or possibly 3) hours. The exam is comprehensive, and a mark of 40% or higher must be obtained to pass the course. Else, the highest possible final grade you will receive is 47%.
